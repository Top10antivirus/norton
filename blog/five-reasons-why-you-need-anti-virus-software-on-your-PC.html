Five Reasons Why You Need Anti-virus Software On Your Computer

Anti-virus software is often ignored by naive users who use desktop PCs or Macs. Many do not see the important role they play in keeping a PC system healthy - including your very private content. In the 21st century, millions of people use the computer to organize their lives and personal affairs. This involves considerable dangers that should not be overlooked. Without virus protection for your PC or Mac, your are vulnerable to hackers, fraudsters and cybercriminals.

In this article, we'll focus on the top five reasons why you should consider getting premium antivirus software.

1. Computer performance


A computer that is infected with viruses, malware and Trojans is significantly slower than a healthy system. Viruses can eat into precious disk space, block important programs and even block access to the Internet. A lethargic system can make it impossible to use, and even the lightest use cases take minutes or even hours. Here are some of the possible signs that your PC is infected by a virus: Windows takes a few minutes to start, even though you have usable / modern hardware; Internet data transmission is very slow despite a good connection; The PC often gets stuck for no apparent reason and needs to be restarted etc.

Anti-virus software can prevent unwanted access to the computer system and blocks attempts to install the annoyances that cause the problems described above. Regularly scanning the computer is also important to make sure nothing slips through the barrier.

2. Protect your kids online


The Internet is not a safe place for children without the help of antivirus software. The little ones innocently search their favorite websites and can easily be redirected to pornographic websites. Then they are exposed to unsuitable material by viruses that camouflage themselves as search result lists and advertisements.

Many parents understandably care about the well-being of children in the playground, the street and even in their own garden. But they neglect their security on unprotected computer systems. If your kids regularly use your devices, you need a proper antivirus program to protect them.

3. Abuse of personal information and identity theft


Cybercrime is one of the fastest growing illegal activities in the world. People use computers to read emails, share documents, and check their accounts online. If you fail to deploy efficient antivirus protection, malicious software can provide access to your system to cybercriminials - and give third parties insight into your personal information.

Certain types of malicious software are now capable of recording keystrokes, allowing would-be hackers easy access to your personal accounts, private photos, credit cards, and more ... Recently, a new virus has been discovered that can take over the webcam without the Owner of the laptop knowing about it. As a result, videos with private and intimate moments of tens of thousands of people were exposed on some Darknet websites.

4. Hard disk failures


Some viruses can wipe your computer's hard drive and disable the computer. This can lead to the loss of important data, which can have a devastating effect on you or your business. Perhaps more worrying, viruses now have the ability to manipulate and modify data on the hard drive without the user knowing.

Buying a new hard drive is not only expensive. At this point, important data can be lost forever. Just think about losing all your family photos taken over the years, or vital work projects... Within moments everything could be gone or stolen.

5. Crime time


Many hackers are now using viruses to get behind firewalls and from there carry out illegal activities with their victim's IP address. This is different from identity theft because the purpose of the virus is not to gain access to the computer. The goal is to commit a crime such as sending junk mail - or to use it as a location for pirated movies. In such cases, the crime caused will point to your home IP address. And you will be suspected of spreading pirated material or worse.

We spend money on safe cars with airbags, helmets, and travel insurance, but while it's obvious that our lives are becoming increasingly digital, many people shy away from spending money on anti-virus software, despite the many dangers described above. The advantages of spending a small sum on a useful antivirus software outweigh the disadvantages clearly.

If you look at the bigger picture, it's absolutely necessary to be safe online and protected from frustrating pop-ups, scams and computer viruses. Do not be reckless with your private information, protect yourself in cyberspace now!
