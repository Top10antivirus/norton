Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Do I still need virus protection on my tablet?

Yes! Remember, everything’s connected. Your computer is synced to your phone and tablet and practically shares the same access to your files and data. Thus, it is crucial to protect your devices, not just your main computer, equally. After all, if one of your devices gets hacked, everything else is compromised. Thankfully, many of our recommended antiviruses cover multiple devices, so you can protect yourself and your family against the latest virus, malware, adware, and other threats!


Don't tablets already come with built-in antivirus protection?

Yes, most tablets do. However, built-in security software rarely gets updates, so it can't keep up with rapidly evolving threats. Besides, it can't protect you against phishing, identity theft, ransomware, and privacy leaks.


How can my tablet get infected with malware?

There are two common ways to expose your tablet against malware, viruses, Trojans, and other security threats: apps and downloads. When you click an unknown link in your email or open a pop-up ad on a website, cybercriminals can automatically download a malicious file on your device without you even noticing. Downloading apps from third-party stores also exposes you to malware-laden apps that hackers use to steal your password, delete files, and more.


Can I get rid of viruses by doing a factory reset?

Well, a factory reset is one way of potentially removing viruses and malware, but it doesn't work 100% of the time. Also, doing a factory reset does not merely get rid of potential threats. It gets your phone back to the same state when it first left the factory, erasing photos, videos, files, and saved settings. Why not install decent antivirus software instead? This way, you won't even have to worry about your tablet getting infected in the first place!


How do I know if an antivirus program is compatible with my tablet?

The top antivirus apps we recommend on this page are all compatible with major tablet brands, including but not limited to: Samsung Galaxy Tab, Apple iPad, Lenovo, Amazon Fire, Huawei, and Xiaomi.
